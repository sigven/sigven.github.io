About

I am a research scientist working at the Institute for Cancer Research, Oslo University Hospital, Norway. Most of my research activities involve various types of cancer data science. I am currently affiliated with the Centre for Cancer Cell Reprogramming (CanCell), as well as the Centre for Bioinformatics at the Department of Informatics, University of Oslo. My background is in computer science from NTNU/University of Minnesota, and I received my PhD in computational biology from the Centre for Molecular Biology and Neuroscience at the University of Oslo.

Research

I develop computational interpretation frameworks and analyze data from high-throughput DNA sequencing technologies. Below are some of my ongoing research activities:

  • Cancer genome interpretation
    • Development of decision support tools for precision cancer medicine: Personal Cancer Genome Reporter (PCGR)
  • Data integration for cancer systems biology
    • Gene set interpretation in high-throughput cancer biology with oncoEnrichR
  • Computational modeling of drug response in cancer
  • Natural language processing (NLP) of clinical trial descriptions
  • Hereditary cancers - risk assessment and variant interpretation
    • Cancer Predisposition Sequencing Reporter (CPSR)
    • Risk assessment in Lynch Syndrome

I supervise MSc students at the Department of Informatics, University of Oslo, which have included projects on:

  • text mining of clinical trial data
  • evaluation of methods for cancer driver gene prediction
  • cluster analysis of data from genome-wide association studies
  • comparison of somatic and germline mutational spectra
  • mutational signatures in cancer - relationship to drug response and survival

Papers I have co-authored can be found on my Google Scholar page.
